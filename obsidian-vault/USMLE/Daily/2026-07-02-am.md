## 🚀 Rapid Fire Recap (High Yield Summary)
1. **Scenario: Pregnant, large uterus, BP 150/90 at 12 weeks**
    - **Diagnosis:** Hydatidiform Mole (Molar Pregnancy)
    - **Key Action:** Transvaginal Ultrasound ("Snowstorm"), Suction D&C
2. **Post-treatment Surveillance for Mole**
    - **Rule:** Weekly β-hCG to zero → Monthly x 6 months.
    - **Must-Do:** Reliable Contraception (Pills, etc.) to distinguish rising β-hCG from new pregnancy vs. malignancy.
3. **Scenario: 6 months postpartum, hemoptysis (coughing blood), vaginal nodule**
    - **Diagnosis:** Choriocarcinoma
    - **Key Trap:** DO NOT Biopsy the vaginal mass (bleeding risk).
    - **Key Action:** Check β-hCG, Start Chemotherapy (Methotrexate).
4. **Hyperemesis Criteria**
    - **Facts:** >5% Weight loss + Ketonuria.
    - **Metabolic Lab:** Hypochloremic Metabolic Alkalosis + Hypokalemia.
5. **Safety First in Hyperemesis**
    - **Trap:** Giving D5W (Sugar water) before vitamins.
    - **Fact:** Thiamine BEFORE Glucose to prevent Wernicke's.
